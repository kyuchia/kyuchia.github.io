Ashes to Ashes: An Immersive Soundscape Installation of Keelung Heping Island

Research | 2023 (Paper submitted)

Interpret the history of the East Asian seas through the auditory dimension.
Integrate music technology, archaeoacoustic, and musicology to reshape the ancient sonic world.


Abstract

This research explores the crossroads of archaeoacoustics and music technology, unveiling the rich acoustic history of ancient civilizations. Using Heping Island, Taiwan, as a case study, it delves into the creative process behind "Ashes to Ashes," a composition that fuses flute, percussion, and live electronics [1]. The project combines field recordings, virtual instruments, and historical research to recreate the acoustic landscapes of the past, connecting modern audiences with this historical context in a novel way. Additionally, the research employs physical modeling sound synthesis software to replicate the sounds of prehistoric iron objects and pottery artifacts unearthed at the excavation site, simulating collisions, striking, and the sounds produced when these objects served as improvised instruments. The study also explores technology-assisted music composition techniques, including fragmentation, instrumental re-synthesis, and data sonification. By revitalizing ancient sounds and cultural elements, this work bridges the gap between modern music and the general public, fostering awareness of archaeology and cultural heritage.



Author: Tak-Cheung Hui and Yu-Chia Kuo
